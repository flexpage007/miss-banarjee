To the girl who
made everything make sense

I've thought a thousand times about how to say this, and every time I come back to the same simple truth — you changed something in me that I didn't even know needed changing. Before you, days were just days. Now they carry your name, your laugh, the colour of your eyes when something makes you genuinely happy.

Today you turn eighteen, and I want you to know that this isn't just a number. It's the beginning of everything you've been quietly preparing for — the adventures, the dreams, the version of yourself that's still unfolding. And I am so lucky, so endlessly grateful, to get a seat at the front row of your story.

I love the way you care — deeply, honestly, without conditions. I love your mind, the way it works, the way you see things differently. I love your quiet moments just as much as your loud ones. I love that you're soft and strong at the same time, like the ocean — gentle on the surface but with something vast and limitless underneath.

There are so many futures I want to share with you. Lazy morning walks and late-night conversations that stretch until we forget what time it is. Every small, ordinary moment made extraordinary just because you're in it. I dream about all of it. I dream about you.

So here's my birthday wish for you — may this year be the year where everything you've worked for starts to bloom. May you feel loved every single day, especially on the days that are hard. May you never forget how rare and wonderful you are. And may you always know that someone out here loves you more than words can hold.
